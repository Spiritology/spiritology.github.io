### Post 5: What Actually Happens to Your Brain During Meditation

Let's talk neuroscience for a second.

When you meditate consistently for 8+ weeks, brain scans show measurable changes:

**Prefrontal Cortex** — thickens. This is your center for decision-making, focus, and emotional regulation. You literally become better at choosing your responses.

**Amygdala** — shrinks. The brain's fear center reduces in volume. You become less reactive to stress, not because you suppress it — but because the wiring changes.

**Default Mode Network** — quiets down. This is the brain network responsible for rumination, self-referential thinking, and the "monkey mind." Less activity here = more presence.

**Gamma Wave Activity** — increases. Associated with heightened awareness, insight, and what researchers call "flow states."

This isn't belief. This is biology.
