About Spiritology

A Technology of the Spirit

Spiritology is the study and handling of the spirit in relation to itself, other spirits, and the entirety of the physical universe. It encompasses a body of knowledge and a precise technology for the rehabilitation of the human spirit — restoring to it capabilities, certainties, and freedoms that have been diminished through accumulated trauma, unconscious programming, and the reactive patterns that drive human behavior.

The word derives from the Latin spiritus (spirit, breath, the animating force of life) and the Greek logos (knowledge, reason, the ordered study of). It means, precisely: the study of the spirit, or knowing about knowing.

Spiritology was founded in 1971 by Dr. Elias Voss following two decades of cross-disciplinary research spanning neuroscience, quantum physics, contemplative traditions, indigenous healing systems, and the philosophy of mind. His discovery: that beneath every authentic tradition of inner transformation — ancient and modern, Eastern and Western, scientific and mystical — lies a single, universal architecture of spiritual liberation.

This architecture is precise. It is sequential. It is teachable. And its results are consistent and verifiable. Spiritology is not a faith. It is a technology — applied with the same exactness as any engineering discipline, producing the same predictable results in any individual who walks the path correctly.

Core Doctrine

The Fundamental Truths

The axioms upon which the entire technology of Spiritology is built. These are not articles of faith — they are discoverable realities, verifiable through personal experience.

I. The Spirit

You are an immortal spiritual being — a Spirit. You are not your body. You are not your brain. You are not the voice in your head. You are the awareness behind all of it. The Spirit is the source of consciousness, will, and identity. It has existed before this life and will continue beyond it.

II. The Reactive Mind

The reactive mind is a stimulus-response mechanism that records every moment of pain, loss, and unconsciousness experienced across the entirety of a being's existence. These recordings — called imprints — operate below awareness and produce compulsive behavior, irrational fears, psychosomatic illness, and the suppression of spiritual ability.

III. Processing

Processing is the central practice of Spiritology: a precise, structured procedure through which imprints are located, examined, and permanently erased from the reactive mind. Conducted between a trained Processor and a practitioner, it follows an exact protocol. The result is measurable: increased clarity, reduced reactivity, restored self-determination.

IV. The Eight Dynamics

All existence operates through eight drives toward survival: Self, Family, Groups, Humanity, Life Forms, the Physical Universe, the Spiritual Universe, and Infinity. Spiritual health means balanced flourishing across all eight. Imbalance in any dynamic indicates reactive patterns requiring processing.

V. The Bridge

The Bridge to Total Freedom is the complete, sequential map of consciousness states from human bondage to total spiritual liberation. It consists of precisely ordered levels, each with specific criteria for completion. It is not theoretical — it is a working route walked by millions.

VI. Self-Determined Ethics

Ethical conduct is not imposed from outside. It arises naturally as the reactive mind is cleared. A being operating from the Spirit — rather than reactive compulsion — acts in accordance with the highest good across all dynamics. Spiritology does not enforce morality; it restores the conditions under which morality is natural.

Understanding Spiritology

What is Spiritology?

Spiritology is a body of knowledge and a technology of the spirit. Developed by Dr. Elias Voss over a twenty-year period of research spanning neuroscience, contemplative traditions, quantum physics, and indigenous healing systems, it provides a complete and precise methodology for understanding and rehabilitating the human spirit.

The central discovery of Spiritology is the reactive mind — a previously unidentified mechanism of the mind that records moments of pain, loss, and unconsciousness, and then uses these recordings to exert automatic control over a being's behavior, emotions, and perceptions. This mechanism operates entirely below awareness. It is the source of irrational fears, compulsive behaviors, psychosomatic conditions, and the suppression of spiritual ability.

Spiritology provides the technology — called Processing — to systematically locate, examine, and erase these recordings. The result is a being increasingly free from reactive compulsion, operating from self-determined choice, and restored to the natural capacities of the Spirit.

Background and Origins

▾

The origins of Spiritology trace to the late 1950s, when the young researcher Elias Voss — then pursuing parallel studies in neuroscience and the philosophy of mind at the University of Vienna — began to notice a pattern that would define his life's work.

Across every authentic tradition of inner transformation he studied — from Zen Buddhism to Advaitic Vedanta, from Christian mysticism to Amazonian plant medicine traditions, from Jungian depth psychology to emerging contemplative neuroscience — he found the same underlying architecture: a precise sequence of stages through which a human being moves from reactive unconsciousness to self-determined awareness.

The specific methods varied. The cultural packaging differed. But the deep structure was invariant. Voss called this the Universal Architecture of Consciousness — and dedicated the next two decades to mapping it completely.

"I did not invent the technology of spiritual freedom. I discovered it — in the same way a physicist discovers a law of nature. It was always there. I simply mapped it precisely enough to make it teachable."
— Dr. Elias Voss, 1971

In 1971, following research across 14 countries and study with hundreds of teachers, Voss published Spiritology: The Science of the Spirit — the foundational text of the field — and opened the first Church of Spiritology in San Francisco with 40 founding members.

The Reactive Mind: A Detailed Explanation

▾

What is the Reactive Mind?

The reactive mind is a stimulus-response mechanism present in every human being. It operates entirely below the level of conscious awareness. Unlike the analytical mind — which reasons, evaluates, and decides — the reactive mind does not think. It reacts. It records and replays.

How Does It Work?

Whenever a being experiences a moment of pain, loss, extreme stress, or unconsciousness (whether physical or emotional), the reactive mind creates a recording of that moment. This recording — called an imprint — captures everything present in the environment during the incident: the physical sensations, the emotions, the words spoken, the sights, sounds, and smells.

These imprints are stored permanently. They do not fade with time. And they exert their influence automatically: whenever elements from the original incident are encountered again in present-time, the imprint reactivates and produces a compulsive reaction — an emotion, a physical sensation, a behavior — that the person experiences as "just who I am."

Examples

  • A child who is ridiculed while speaking may develop a lifelong fear of public speaking — not because of rational evaluation, but because the imprint of humiliation reactivates whenever similar conditions arise.
  • A person who experienced a car accident in the rain may feel unexplained anxiety whenever they drive in rain — the reactive mind has linked "rain" to "danger" in a recording they cannot consciously access.
  • An adult who repeatedly chooses emotionally unavailable partners may be replaying an imprint of early parental absence — unconsciously recreating the familiar pattern because the reactive mind treats familiarity as safety.

The Key Discovery

Dr. Voss's critical breakthrough was the discovery that these imprints can be precisely located, examined in a controlled setting, and permanently erased from the reactive mind through the technology of Processing. When an imprint is fully contacted and processed, it loses its compulsive power permanently. The memory remains available to the analytical mind, but it no longer drives automatic behavior.

Processing: How It Works

▾

Processing is the central practice of Spiritology. It is a structured procedure conducted between two people: the Processor (a trained practitioner) and the Seeker (the person receiving processing).

The Setting

Processing takes place in a private, quiet room. The Seeker sits comfortably. The Processor sits opposite, using a device called a Spiritual Monitor (S-Monitor) — a sensitive instrument that measures subtle changes in the body's electrical resistance. These changes correspond to the activation and release of imprints in the reactive mind.

The Procedure

The Processor asks precise questions drawn from the exact processing protocols developed by Dr. Voss. These questions are designed to direct the Seeker's attention toward specific areas of the reactive mind. The Seeker answers honestly, reporting whatever comes to mind.

When an imprint is contacted, the S-Monitor registers a characteristic response. The Processor guides the Seeker to examine the imprint fully — the sensations, emotions, and content of the recording — until it discharges completely. This discharge is both subjectively felt (relief, clarity, sometimes laughter or tears) and objectively measured (the S-Monitor returns to a specific baseline reading).

The Result

A fully processed imprint loses its compulsive power permanently. The Seeker retains the memory but is no longer controlled by it. Over the course of systematic processing, the reactive mind is progressively cleared, and the Spirit is restored to increasing levels of freedom, ability, and self-determination.

The Bridge to Total Freedom

▾

The Bridge is the complete map of consciousness states from ordinary human awareness to total spiritual freedom. It consists of precisely ordered levels, grouped into two major divisions:

The Lower Bridge (Levels I–III): Clearing

  • Level I — The Awakening: Recognition of the Spirit. Introduction to basic concepts. Spiritual Evaluation. First processing sessions addressing surface-level reactive patterns.
  • Level II — Clarity: Systematic clearing of the major categories of reactive programming: loss, misidentification, communication blocks, and misaligned purposes. Restoration of emotional equilibrium and mental clarity.
  • Level III — Liberation (Clear): The state of Clear is achieved when the individual no longer has their own reactive mind operating against their self-determined wishes. A Clear can think, act, and perceive without reactive compulsion. This is the first major milestone on the Bridge.

The Upper Bridge (Levels IV–VIII): Operating Spirit

  • Level IV — Ascension: Addresses reactive patterns shared across beings and lifetimes. The Spirit begins to operate with capabilities beyond ordinary human experience.
  • Level V — Sovereignty: Complete mastery over the reactive mechanism. The Spirit exists in full cause over its own existence.
  • Level VI–VIII — Transcendence: The highest attainable states. Available only at Advanced Spiritual Centers. Content is confidential to protect the spiritual experience of those walking the path.

Spiritology Training & Study

▾

Processing addresses the reactive mind directly. Training provides the intellectual and practical knowledge to understand how the mind works, how the technology operates, and how to apply it to others.

Spiritology training follows a precise sequence of courses, each building on the last:

  • Basic Study Course: Fundamentals of learning and study technique.
  • Spiritology Fundamentals: The core principles, axioms, and definitions.
  • Communication Course: Mastery of the fundamental components of communication.
  • Processor Training Levels I–XII: Progressive certification in processing technology, from introductory to expert levels.
  • Specialist Courses: Ethics, organizational technology, advanced study materials.

All training materials were authored by Dr. Voss and are applied exactly as written. The technology is not interpreted, adapted, or modified — it is used precisely as developed, ensuring consistent results across all practitioners worldwide.

Questions

Frequently Asked

What does "Spiritology" mean? ▾

The word Spiritology comes from the Latin spiritus (spirit, breath, the life force) and the Greek logos (knowledge, reason, study). It literally means "the study of the spirit" or "knowing about knowing." It was coined by Dr. Elias Voss in 1971 to describe the precise, systematic study of the human spirit and its rehabilitation.

Is Spiritology a religion? ▾

Yes. The Church of Spiritology is a recognized religious organization. However, unlike faith-based religions, Spiritology does not ask its members to accept anything on faith alone. Its principles are presented as testable propositions that each individual can verify through their own experience. Spiritology is compatible with other religious beliefs — many members also practice other faiths.

How much does it cost? ▾

The Spiritual Evaluation is free. Introductory services and Sunday ceremonies are free. Structured programs begin at $499 for Level I (The Awakening). A complete breakdown of all service fees is available at any Church. The Church also offers payment plans, financial assistance programs, and exchange programs where members contribute service hours in exchange for processing. No one is turned away for financial reasons.

Can I leave the Church? ▾

Yes. Absolutely. At any time. Without consequence. The right to leave freely is enshrined in our Creed and Code of Spiritual Ethics. There is no excommunication, shunning, or disconnection practice. Members who leave remain welcome to return if they choose, and are treated with the same respect as any other person.

What is the Spiritual Evaluation? ▾

The Spiritual Evaluation is a structured self-assessment consisting of 200 questions covering all four layers of consciousness and all eight dynamics of existence. It takes approximately 45 minutes. The results are analyzed by a trained evaluator who provides a personalized consciousness profile and recommended starting point on the Bridge. It is free, confidential, and involves no obligation of any kind.

Is there scientific evidence for Spiritology? ▾

Spiritology draws on established findings from neuroscience (neuroplasticity, the Default Mode Network, trauma encoding), biophysics (heart coherence, bioelectromagnetic fields), and psychology (subconscious conditioning, cognitive behavioral patterns). The specific technology of Processing has been the subject of internal clinical studies since 1975, with published results available through the Church's research division. Independent academic research is ongoing at several universities.

How is Spiritology organized? ▾

The Church is organized hierarchically. At the top is Church of Spiritology International (CSI), the mother church. Below it are Continental Offices (Americas, Europe, Africa, Asia-Pacific), National Organizations, and individual Churches and Missions. Each church is locally managed but adheres to the standard technology and ethics protocols established by CSI. The Founder, Dr. Elias Voss, serves as the spiritual leader. The Board of Directors manages administrative operations.

What about controversies? ▾

Like any significant religious movement, Spiritology has faced criticism over its five decades. The Church takes all legitimate concerns seriously and has implemented multiple reforms since its founding, including the establishment of an independent Ethics Review Board (2001), a formal Whistleblower Protection Policy (2008), mandatory financial transparency reporting (2012), and the public affirmation that all members have the unconditional right to leave (reaffirmed annually since 1972). The Church welcomes genuine inquiry and maintains an open-door policy for journalists and researchers.

The Founder

Dr. Elias Voss

Researcher, author, spiritual pioneer. b. 1943, Vienna, Austria.

Born to a family of academics in post-war Vienna, Elias Voss demonstrated an extraordinary capacity for inquiry from early childhood. By 19, he was studying across three universities simultaneously — pursuing neuroscience, philosophy, and comparative religion with equal intensity.

His academic career took him through the most rigorous institutions of Western science. Yet it was experiences outside the laboratory — in Zen monasteries in Japan, with Amazonian healers in Peru, in Tibetan meditation retreats, and at the intersection of quantum physics and consciousness research — that catalyzed his breakthrough discovery.

Between 1962 and 1971, Voss traveled to 14 countries, studied with hundreds of teachers, and conducted over 3,000 hours of documented research. His discovery: that beneath every authentic system of inner transformation lies a single, universal architecture — a precise sequence of states and processes that is invariant across cultures, traditions, and historical periods.

In 1971, he published Spiritology: The Science of the Spirit and founded the first Church. Over the next five decades, he authored more than 40 books, delivered over 3,000 lectures, and developed the complete technical materials of the Bridge to Total Freedom.

"The spirit does not need to be saved. It needs to be freed. And the technology to free it is exact."

Dr. Voss continues to guide the Church from the International Headquarters in Clearfield, California. He is 82 years old.
